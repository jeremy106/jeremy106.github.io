Te Whare Tapa Wha

Wellbeing Plan

Te whare tapa whā is an approach to health that combines the four pillars of taha tinana (physical wellbeing), taha hinengaro (mental wellbeing), taha wairua (spiritual wellbeing), taha whānau (family wellbeing) together with a strong connection with the whenua (land) to create a resilient overall whole. My approaches to strengthening each pilllar are as follows:

Taha Tinana

I have a strong background in sport and exercise and I intend to maintain my running, swimming, and gym training throughout my learning journey.

Taha Whānau

It is a tradition in our household to eat dinner together at the table every evening as a family. No screens and no distractions.

Taha Hinengaro

I practice mindfulness while taking my dog for a walk. Often this is out in nature which also strengthens te whenua.

Taha Wairua

My approach to Taha Wairua is my own

Whenua

We make a point to walk down the nearby river, or in the bush every weekend. I take great pleasure in spotting creatures in the undergrowth


Bootcamp Update

During bootcamp I have had an opportunity to reflect on the plan outlined above. I have felt energetic, motivated, and happy throughout my time so far and don't feel the need to change anything about how I am looking after myself.
